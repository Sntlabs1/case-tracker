Systemic Violation Report

Portfolio Recovery Associates  ·  FCRA furnisher dispute-pattern screening  ·  prepared 2026-06-18
Screening analysis — not a liability finding. This report identifies and sizes a consumer population and a dispute pattern worth investigating. It does not establish that any account was inaccurately reported or that Portfolio Recovery Associates failed to reasonably reinvestigate — the elements of an FCRA claim, which require individual merits verification (test disputes, discovery into dispute-handling procedures, accuracy sampling). The dispute figures are a screening signal, not proof.

Executive summary

Across a 5,528,402-consumer national credit panel, Portfolio Recovery Associates furnished 1,329,557 tradelines to 508,389 distinct consumers. Of those tradelines, 72.2% were marked disputed yet continued to report — a population-wide dispute pattern that no single-firm client set can observe.

The pattern to investigate: 423,461 consumers disputed a Portfolio Recovery Associates tradeline that continued to report; 919,964 of those disputed tradelines were still reporting a positive balance. 37,717 consumers have a Portfolio Recovery Associates tradeline reported on/after 2024 — the reporting is current, so claims arising from it would be timely. Whether this pattern reflects inadequate reinvestigation is the merits question to test.

Recovery exposure (illustrative ceiling — if liability is established)

FCRA § 1681n provides statutory damages of $100–$1,000 per willful violation. If liability were established across the 423,461 consumers in this screen, statutory exposure would range $42.3M – $423.5M, before actual or punitive damages and fees. This is an upper-bound scoping figure — not a prediction of liability or an expected recovery.

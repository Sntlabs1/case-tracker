Systemic Violation Report

First Premier Bank  ·  FCRA furnisher dispute-pattern screening  ·  prepared 2026-06-18
Screening analysis — not a liability finding. This report identifies and sizes a consumer population and a dispute pattern worth investigating. It does not establish that any account was inaccurately reported or that First Premier Bank failed to reasonably reinvestigate — the elements of an FCRA claim, which require individual merits verification (test disputes, discovery into dispute-handling procedures, accuracy sampling). The dispute figures are a screening signal, not proof.

Executive summary

Across a 5,528,402-consumer national credit panel, First Premier Bank furnished 2,634,655 tradelines to 971,687 distinct consumers. Of those tradelines, 30.2% were marked disputed yet continued to report — a population-wide dispute pattern that no single-firm client set can observe.

The pattern to investigate: 421,237 consumers disputed a First Premier Bank tradeline that continued to report; 651,474 of those disputed tradelines were still reporting a positive balance. 83,503 consumers have a First Premier Bank tradeline reported on/after 2024 — the reporting is current, so claims arising from it would be timely. Whether this pattern reflects inadequate reinvestigation is the merits question to test.

Candidate class (for investigation)

Should the disputes reflect inaccurate reporting or inadequate reinvestigation — which requires merits verification — the candidate class would be: all natural persons in the United States as to whom First Premier Bank furnished a consumer-account tradeline that the consumer disputed and which First Premier Bank thereafter continued to report, during the applicable limitations period. The panel identifies at least 421,237 consumers fitting this screen — a numerosity indicator; whether common questions as to First Premier Bank's reinvestigation practices predominate is the merits question to be tested.

Recovery exposure (illustrative ceiling — if liability is established)

FCRA § 1681n provides statutory damages of $100–$1,000 per willful violation. If liability were established across the 421,237 consumers in this screen, statutory exposure would range $42.1M – $421.2M, before actual or punitive damages and fees. This is an upper-bound scoping figure — not a prediction of liability or an expected recovery.

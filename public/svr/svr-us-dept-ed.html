Systemic Violation Report

Us Dept Ed  ·  FCRA furnisher dispute-pattern screening  ·  prepared 2026-06-18
Screening analysis — not a liability finding. This report identifies and sizes a consumer population and a dispute pattern worth investigating. It does not establish that any account was inaccurately reported or that Us Dept Ed failed to reasonably reinvestigate — the elements of an FCRA claim, which require individual merits verification (test disputes, discovery into dispute-handling procedures, accuracy sampling). The dispute figures are a screening signal, not proof.

Executive summary

Across a 5,528,402-consumer national credit panel, Us Dept Ed furnished 747,573 tradelines to 199,188 distinct consumers. Of those tradelines, 35.7% were marked disputed yet continued to report — a population-wide dispute pattern that no single-firm client set can observe.

The pattern to investigate: 100,781 consumers disputed a Us Dept Ed tradeline that continued to report; 207,472 of those disputed tradelines were still reporting a positive balance. 9,419 consumers have a Us Dept Ed tradeline reported on/after 2024 — the reporting is current, so claims arising from it would be timely. Whether this pattern reflects inadequate reinvestigation is the merits question to test.

Candidate class (for investigation)

Should the disputes reflect inaccurate reporting or inadequate reinvestigation — which requires merits verification — the candidate class would be: all natural persons in the United States as to whom Us Dept Ed furnished a consumer-account tradeline that the consumer disputed and which Us Dept Ed thereafter continued to report, during the applicable limitations period. The panel identifies at least 100,781 consumers fitting this screen — a numerosity indicator; whether common questions as to Us Dept Ed's reinvestigation practices predominate is the merits question to be tested.

Recovery exposure (illustrative ceiling — if liability is established)

FCRA § 1681n provides statutory damages of $100–$1,000 per willful violation. If liability were established across the 100,781 consumers in this screen, statutory exposure would range $10.1M – $100.8M, before actual or punitive damages and fees. This is an upper-bound scoping figure — not a prediction of liability or an expected recovery.

Methodology & provenance. Aggregate analysis of a 5,528,402-consumer national credit panel (tradeline-level, last reported through May 2026). Furnisher spellings collapsed to one defendant via canonical normalization (1 source spellings). "Disputed" = the bureau dispute compliance indicator on the tradeline — evidence a dispute was filed and the item continued to report. It is a screening signal, not proof that the information was inaccurate or that reinvestigation was unreasonable; establishing an FCRA violation requires individual merits verification (test disputes, discovery into dispute-handling procedures, accuracy sampling). The double-sold and obsolete-reporting figures are closer to objective, record-based inaccuracy. Docket counts from PACER. No personally identifying information is contained in this report.

Systemic Violation Report

Verizon Wireless  ·  FCRA furnisher dispute-pattern screening  ·  prepared 2026-06-18
Screening analysis — not a liability finding. This report identifies and sizes a consumer population and a dispute pattern worth investigating. It does not establish that any account was inaccurately reported or that Verizon Wireless failed to reasonably reinvestigate — the elements of an FCRA claim, which require individual merits verification (test disputes, discovery into dispute-handling procedures, accuracy sampling). The dispute figures are a screening signal, not proof.

Executive summary

Across a 5,528,402-consumer national credit panel, Verizon Wireless furnished 666,376 tradelines to 347,032 distinct consumers. Of those tradelines, 67.3% were marked disputed yet continued to report — a population-wide dispute pattern that no single-firm client set can observe.

The pattern to investigate: 267,613 consumers disputed a Verizon Wireless tradeline that continued to report; 337,984 of those disputed tradelines were still reporting a positive balance. 40,909 consumers have a Verizon Wireless tradeline reported on/after 2024 — the reporting is current, so claims arising from it would be timely. Whether this pattern reflects inadequate reinvestigation is the merits question to test.

Candidate class (for investigation)

Should the disputes reflect inaccurate reporting or inadequate reinvestigation — which requires merits verification — the candidate class would be: all natural persons in the United States as to whom Verizon Wireless furnished a consumer-account tradeline that the consumer disputed and which Verizon Wireless thereafter continued to report, during the applicable limitations period. The panel identifies at least 267,613 consumers fitting this screen — a numerosity indicator; whether common questions as to Verizon Wireless's reinvestigation practices predominate is the merits question to be tested.

Recovery exposure (illustrative ceiling — if liability is established)

FCRA § 1681n provides statutory damages of $100–$1,000 per willful violation. If liability were established across the 267,613 consumers in this screen, statutory exposure would range $26.8M – $267.6M, before actual or punitive damages and fees. This is an upper-bound scoping figure — not a prediction of liability or an expected recovery.

Methodology & provenance. Aggregate analysis of a 5,528,402-consumer national credit panel (tradeline-level, last reported through May 2026). Furnisher spellings collapsed to one defendant via canonical normalization (1 source spellings). "Disputed" = the bureau dispute compliance indicator on the tradeline — evidence a dispute was filed and the item continued to report.

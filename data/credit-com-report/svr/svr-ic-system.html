Systemic Violation Report

Ic System  ·  FCRA § 1681i furnisher analysis  ·  prepared 2026-06-17

Executive summary

Across a 5,528,402-consumer national credit panel, Ic System furnished 356,933 tradelines to 286,809 distinct consumers. Of those tradelines, 57.3% were marked disputed yet continued to report — a population-wide pattern of furnishing after dispute that no single-plaintiff record set can establish.

The systemic signal: 177,173 consumers disputed a Ic System tradeline; 203,259 of those disputed tradelines were still reporting a positive balance. 23,245 consumers have a Ic System tradeline reported on/after 2024 — the violation is current, not historical.

Proposed class definition

All natural persons in the United States as to whom Ic System furnished a consumer-account tradeline to a nationwide credit reporting agency that the consumer disputed and which Ic System thereafter continued to report, during the applicable limitations period. Panel evidence shows a class of at least 177,173 such consumers (numerosity), with common questions as to Ic System's reinvestigation and furnishing practices (commonality / predominance).

Recovery exposure (illustrative ceiling)

FCRA § 1681n provides statutory damages of $100–$1,000 per willful violation. Applied to the 177,173 consumers with a disputed Ic System tradeline, statutory exposure ranges $17.7M – $177.2M, before actual or punitive damages and fees. A ceiling for scoping, not an expected recovery.

Executive summary

Across a 5,528,402-consumer national credit panel, Americollect furnished 546,734 tradelines to 100,678 distinct consumers. Of those tradelines, 47.2% were marked disputed yet continued to report — a population-wide pattern of furnishing after dispute that no single-plaintiff record set can establish.

The systemic signal: 78,048 consumers disputed a Americollect tradeline; 250,261 of those disputed tradelines were still reporting a positive balance. 1,595 consumers have a Americollect tradeline reported on/after 2024 — the violation is current, not historical.

Proposed class definition

All natural persons in the United States as to whom Americollect furnished a consumer-account tradeline to a nationwide credit reporting agency that the consumer disputed and which Americollect thereafter continued to report, during the applicable limitations period. Recovery exposure (illustrative ceiling)

FCRA § 1681n provides statutory damages of $100–$1,000 per willful violation. Applied to the 78,048 consumers with a disputed Americollect tradeline, statutory exposure ranges $7.8M – $78.0M, before actual or punitive damages and fees. A ceiling for scoping, not an expected recovery.

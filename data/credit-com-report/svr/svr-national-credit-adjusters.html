Executive summary

Across a 5,528,402-consumer national credit panel, National Credit Adjusters furnished 257,007 tradelines to 140,068 distinct consumers. Of those tradelines, 68.6% were marked disputed yet continued to report — a population-wide pattern of furnishing after dispute that no single-plaintiff record set can establish.

The systemic signal: 109,448 consumers disputed a National Credit Adjusters tradeline; 158,334 of those disputed tradelines were still reporting a positive balance. 17,540 consumers have a National Credit Adjusters tradeline reported on/after 2024 — the violation is current, not historical.

Proposed class definition

All natural persons in the United States as to whom National Credit Adjusters furnished a consumer-account tradeline to a nationwide credit reporting agency that the consumer disputed and which National Credit Adjusters thereafter continued to report, during the applicable limitations period. Recovery exposure (illustrative ceiling)

FCRA § 1681n provides statutory damages of $100–$1,000 per willful violation. Applied to the 109,448 consumers with a disputed National Credit Adjusters tradeline, statutory exposure ranges $10.9M – $109.4M, before actual or punitive damages and fees. A ceiling for scoping, not an expected recovery.
